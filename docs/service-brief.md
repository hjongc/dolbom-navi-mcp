# Family Care Navigator Service Brief

## Working Name

Family Care Navigator

Korean name candidates:

- 가족돌봄비서
- 돌봄내비
- 케어내비
- 부모님돌봄비서

Recommended contest-facing name: `돌봄내비`.

## Origin Story

The service starts from a real family problem: adult children often watch their parents struggle to find reliable information while caring for grandparents or elderly family members. Nursing homes, dementia-related processes, and long-term care grades are one vivid example, but the broader problem includes welfare benefits, medical support, daily care, mobility, public services, and local senior programs.

The problem is not lack of information. The problem is that the information is fragmented across institutions, hard to interpret for a family's exact situation, and difficult to turn into the next action.

## Target User

Primary user:

- Adult children helping parents care for grandparents or elderly relatives.

Secondary user:

- Parents who need to find welfare, care, medical, mobility, facility, or administrative support for elderly family members.

## Core Problem

Families facing elder-care and senior-support decisions need to answer practical questions quickly:

- What support or benefit might apply to this family situation?
- Which institution should we contact first?
- What documents or facts should we prepare?
- Is this a welfare, medical, long-term care, mobility, facility, or local-government issue?
- How do we compare options without relying only on ads or word of mouth?
- How do we explain the situation to siblings or family members clearly?

Current substitutes are fragmented:

- Search portals
- Government and insurance sites
- Local community posts
- Phone calls to care facilities
- Word-of-mouth recommendations
- Ad-heavy nursing home directories

## Value Promise

`돌봄내비` turns a confusing family care situation into a source-backed action plan.

It helps a family describe the elderly person's situation, identify the relevant support category, understand likely official pathways, compare available options, and create a family-shareable checklist for the next step.

## Contest Thesis

This service fits AGENTIC PLAYER 10 because it is:

- Creative: it applies agentic AI to a painful but under-served family care and senior support workflow.
- Convenient: it converts scattered official and local information into a situation-specific checklist inside Kakao-style conversation.
- Stable: it can rely on public and official sources, avoids sensitive personal identifiers, and shows uncertainty instead of pretending to make legal or medical decisions.

## MVP User Journey

1. User describes the elder care situation in natural language.
   - Example: "Grandmother is 82, has suspected dementia, lives in Seoul, and the family is considering a nursing home."
2. The service extracts non-sensitive care context.
   - Age range, region, dementia status, mobility, living situation, urgent care need, current long-term care grade status.
3. The service identifies likely next paths.
   - Welfare and benefit discovery
   - Medical and dementia support navigation
   - Long-term care recognition and daily care support
   - Mobility and transportation support
   - Facility comparison and consultation preparation
   - Family discussion checklist
4. The service returns source-backed guidance.
   - What to check first
   - Who to contact
   - What documents or facts to prepare
   - What questions to ask facilities
5. The service creates a short family-share summary.
   - A concise KakaoTalk-ready message for family coordination.

## Positioning

Not a medical diagnosis tool.

Not a legal eligibility decision tool.

Not an ad-based facility recommendation service.

It is a family care navigation assistant that helps people ask the right questions, find official paths, and take the next step with less confusion.

## Initial Product Boundary

In scope for MVP:

- Situation intake for elderly-family support needs
- Routing into welfare, medical, care, mobility, facility, or administration categories
- Long-term care and dementia-related process explanation as one important scenario
- Welfare/benefit and local-service checklist where source data exists
- Facility or service comparison checklist
- Region-aware official contact/source suggestions
- Family-share summary
- Source and "confirm with official institution" notices

Out of scope for MVP:

- Medical diagnosis
- Guaranteed eligibility judgment
- Facility or service booking
- Handling resident registration numbers, payment information, or medical records
- Ranking facilities by private review scores
- Emergency medical triage
